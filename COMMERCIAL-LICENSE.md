Skyulf Commercial Terms (Overview)

Skyulf is licensed under a split model: **GNU AGPLv3** for the backend and frontend, and **Apache 2.0** for the standalone `skyulf-core` library.

*   **Backend (AGPLv3):** If you run the backend as a network service (SaaS, internal API, hosted product) and you make any modifications, you must release your **entire application's** source code under AGPLv3. This includes your own code that you combine with Skyulf's backend.
*   **Frontend (AGPLv3):** Same rule — modifications served over a network must be open-sourced.
*   **`skyulf-core` (Apache 2.0):** The standalone Python ML library is permissively licensed. You can use it in proprietary projects without restriction.

**Releases v0.5.15 and earlier** were published under Apache 2.0 and remain under that license. AGPLv3 applies from v0.5.16 onwards.

When would you still contact us?

- **Commercial License Exception:** You want to use the backend or frontend in a proprietary SaaS or product and cannot comply with AGPLv3's copyleft requirements (i.e., you cannot open-source your stack).
- **Enterprise support:** SLAs, roadmap commitments, or long-term maintenance guarantees.
- **Custom licensing:** Bespoke terms required by your legal/compliance team (indemnification, audit clauses, private amendments).
- **Partnerships:** Co-marketing, joint roadmap planning, or early access to unreleased capabilities.
